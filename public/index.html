Serial Port
Auto mode shows the browser port picker on connect. Manual uses a previously authorized port.
Port numbers shown here are assigned by your browser and may not match your system's COM port numbers. To identify which port is which, open Device Manager (Windows) or check ls /dev/ttyUSB* (Linux). For dual-port radios like the FTDX10, the Enhanced port is for CAT control and the Standard port is for audio/digital modes.
TCI Connection
In Thetis: Setup → TCI Server → enable, set port to 50001.

Audio Scope (AF FFT)
Captures USB audio from your radio and displays an audio-frequency (AF) spectrum. Shows 0–24 kHz audio, NOT RF bandwidth. Requires browser microphone permission.
Device names hidden — click "Allow" to grant microphone permission and reveal labels.
AF scope shows audio from the radio's USB sound card. Cannot show signals outside the radio's audio passband. For FTDX10, connect USB audio and select "USB Audio CODEC". Device labels only appear after granting microphone permission once.
